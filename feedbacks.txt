 What could justify the P2P network

  The real question you're circling is: why does anyone need decentralized agent infrastructure instead of just using OpenAI/Anthropic directly?

  Three honest answers:

  1. Cost arbitrage through inference co-ops

  - Small companies and indie devs can't afford dedicated GPU clusters
  - Keepers with idle GPUs sell inference at below-API pricing
  - Siphon routes requests to the cheapest available keeper
  - This is real — inference costs are the #1 blocker for AI startups. A decentralized inference market has genuine demand

  2. Censorship-resistant / uncensorable agents

  - Centralized APIs have content policies that block legitimate use cases (security research, medical advice in underserved regions, controversial but legal content)
  - Self-hosted shards on keeper nodes can run uncensored open-source models
  - The P2P network means no single point of shutdown
  - This is real but politically sensitive to market

  3. Agent persistence and portability

  - Your agent's identity, training history, and reputation travel with you across platforms (ERC-8004)
  - If OpenAI changes their API or shuts down, your agent still exists on the network
  - Keepers provide continuity — your agent is always hosted somewhere
  - This is the strongest unique value prop — no one else is doing portable agent identity

  What about research labs specifically?

  If you really want the academic angle, the play isn't "replace their models" — it's agent labor for repetitive research tasks:

  - Literature review agents that synthesize papers across databases
  - Lab notebook agents that track experimental protocols
  - Grant writing assistants trained on successful proposals
  - Peer review summarizers
  - Data cleaning and annotation agents

  But honestly? That's just a SaaS product. It doesn't need P2P or blockchain. You'd build that as a feature inside Siphon, not as the justification for the network.

  Don't stretch for academic justification. Your P2P network's real justification is #1 (cheap inference market) + #3 (portable agent identity). That's already a strong story:
